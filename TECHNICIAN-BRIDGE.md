# What You Should Already Know

A quick recap of Technician-class concepts that the General exam builds on. If any of these feel rusty, review them before diving into the study guides.

---

## Ohm's Law & Basic Electricity

The General exam assumes you can work with these:

| Formula | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **V = I × R** | Voltage = Current × Resistance |
| **I = V / R** | Current = Voltage / Resistance |
| **R = V / I** | Resistance = Voltage / Current |
| **P = V × I** | Power = Voltage × Current |

**Units:** Volts (V), Amps (A), Ohms (Ω), Watts (W)

The General exam extends this into **reactance** (G5), **impedance** (G5), and **power calculations** like PEP and dB (G5). If you're comfortable with Ohm's Law, the G5 math is just the next step.

---

## Frequency, Wavelength, and Bands

You already know:

- **Frequency × Wavelength = Speed of light** → λ(meters) = 300 / f(MHz)
- VHF = 30–300 MHz, UHF = 300–3000 MHz
- Higher frequency = shorter wavelength = smaller antenna

**What General adds:** HF bands (160m through 10m), band-specific privileges, CW vs. Phone sub-bands, and the 60-meter channelized allocation. See **G1** for the full breakdown.

---

## VHF/UHF Propagation Basics

From Technician, you know:

- VHF/UHF is mostly **line-of-sight** — range depends on antenna height
- **Repeaters** extend range by listening on one frequency and transmitting on another (offset)
- Signals can bounce off buildings, mountains, and aircraft (multipath)
- Weather and terrain affect VHF/UHF propagation

**What General adds:** HF propagation via the **ionosphere** — D, E, and F layers, skip zones, MUF/LUF, solar cycles, and the K-index. This is entirely new territory covered in **G3**. It's one of the most interesting parts of the General exam.

---

## Modulation Modes

From Technician:

- **FM** — frequency modulation, used on VHF/UHF repeaters. Constant amplitude, varying frequency.
- **SSB** — single sideband, briefly introduced. More bandwidth-efficient than FM.

**What General adds:** Deep dive into SSB operating conventions (USB vs. LSB), CW (Morse code), and digital modes like **FT8, PSK31, RTTY, and Winlink**. Covered in **G2** (operating) and **G8** (signals/emissions).

---

## Basic Safety

You already covered:

- Don't touch live circuits
- RF exposure limits exist
- Grounding prevents shock

**What General adds:** Specific **MPE (Maximum Permissible Exposure)** calculations, NEC electrical code requirements (wire gauge for circuits), tower safety procedures, and GFCI protection. See **G0**.

---

## Station Setup

From Technician:

- Transceivers transmit and receive
- SWR should be low (close to 1:1)
- Feed line connects radio to antenna
- A license is required before transmitting

**What General adds:** Deeper understanding of **why** SWR matters, feed line loss calculations, antenna tuners, speech processors, oscilloscopes for testing, and mobile/portable station considerations. Covered in **G4**.

---

## Quick Self-Test

Can you answer these without looking them up?

1. What is the resistance if 12V drives 2A through a circuit? *(6Ω)*
2. What does a higher SWR indicate? *(Worse impedance match)*
3. What's the approximate wavelength of a 146 MHz signal? *(2 meters)*
4. What modulation mode do VHF repeaters typically use? *(FM)*
5. What unit measures electrical resistance? *(Ohms)*

If you got 4-5 right, you're ready to start the General study guides. If not, spend 30 minutes reviewing basic Technician concepts — [ARRL has a free Technician study guide](http://www.arrl.org/getting-your-technician-license).
