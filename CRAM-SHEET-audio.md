# General Class Cram Sheet — Rapid Fire Review

## Key Formulas

Capacitive reactance equals one divided by two pi times frequency times capacitance.

Inductive reactance equals two pi times frequency times inductance.

Resonant frequency equals one divided by two pi times the square root of inductance times capacitance.

Dipole length in feet equals 468 divided by frequency in megahertz.

Transformer relationships. Primary voltage divided by secondary voltage equals primary turns divided by secondary turns, which also equals secondary current divided by primary current.

Power equals current times voltage. Power equals current squared times resistance. Power equals voltage squared divided by resistance.

Peak envelope power equals peak voltage squared divided by two times resistance. Important: use peak voltage, not peak-to-peak voltage.

AC voltage relationships. Peak-to-peak voltage equals two times peak voltage. RMS voltage equals peak voltage times 0.707.

## Modulation Modes

CW has a bandwidth of about 150 hertz and very high efficiency.

SSB has a bandwidth of about 2.4 kilohertz and high efficiency.

AM has a bandwidth of about 6 kilohertz and low efficiency, around 33 percent.

FM has a bandwidth of about 12 to 15 kilohertz and medium efficiency.

## Propagation

The D layer absorbs HF signals during the day and disappears at night.

The E layer supports sporadic E propagation for VHF. A single hop covers about 1,200 miles.

The F2 layer is the main long-distance layer. A single hop covers about 2,500 miles.

Critical frequency is the maximum frequency that reflects straight back down when transmitted vertically.

The maximum usable frequency, or MUF, is about 3 to 4 times the critical frequency.

The skip zone is the area between where the ground wave fades out and where the sky wave comes back down.

The K index measures geomagnetic disturbance. Zero to one means quiet conditions. Five and above means a geomagnetic storm.

NVIS stands for near vertical incidence skywave. Use 40 meters with a low horizontal antenna for regional coverage from zero to about 400 miles.

## Operating Conventions

Use upper sideband on 20 meters and above.

Use lower sideband on 40 meters and below.

CW is found at the lower end of each band.

Digital modes vary by band but typically occupy narrow slices.

## Amplifier Classes

Class A runs at about 25 percent efficiency and is used for audio and low distortion applications.

Class B runs at about 60 percent efficiency and is used in push-pull RF configurations.

Class C runs at about 80 percent efficiency and is used for CW and FM where high efficiency matters.

## Antennas

A dipole has a figure-eight radiation pattern and an impedance of about 72 ohms.

A Yagi is directional. The reflector goes behind the driven element and the directors go in front.

A vertical antenna is omnidirectional and needs radials for a good ground system.

A quarter-wave vertical antenna needs a good ground system to work properly.

## Safety

A ground rod should be at least 8 feet long.

RF exposure follows the inverse square law. Double the distance and the power density drops to one quarter.

For first aid, call 911 and make sure the power is off before touching anything.

Fusing should protect the wire at the source of power.

## RF Connectors

A BNC connector is rated up to 4 gigahertz and is commonly found on test equipment.

A Type N connector is rated up to 10 gigahertz, is weatherproof, and is used at UHF and microwave frequencies.

An SMA connector is rated above 18 gigahertz. It is small and precision-made.

A PL-259 and SO-239 pair is the standard HF connector, sometimes called a UHF connector. It is rated up to about 150 megahertz.

## Quick Conversions

One megahertz equals 1,000 kilohertz.

Wavelength in meters equals 300 divided by frequency in megahertz.

One S unit equals 6 dB, which is a four-times change in power.

## 60 Meter Band — Channelized Operation

The 60 meter band uses five channels. The center frequencies are 5330.5, 5346.5, 5357.0, 5371.5, and 5403.5 kilohertz. All channels allow upper sideband, CW, and digital modes. Maximum power is 100 watts effective radiated power. There is also a 15 kilohertz segment from 5351.5 to 5366.5 kilohertz.
