# Glossary

Key terms from the 2023–2027 General class question pool. Each entry includes a brief definition and the subelement where it's covered in depth.

---

## A

**A-index** — A daily average of geomagnetic activity derived from K-index readings. Higher values indicate more disturbed conditions and worse HF propagation. → G3

**ALC (Automatic Level Control)** — A feedback circuit that prevents excessive drive to the transmitter's final amplifier. Keeps output power consistent and prevents distortion. → G4

**Antenna tuner** — A device that transforms impedance so the transmitter sees 50 ohms. Does NOT reduce SWR on the feed line between tuner and antenna. → G9

**ARQ (Automatic Repeat Request)** — An error-correction method where the receiving station requests retransmission of corrupted data. Used in PACTOR and Winlink. Contrast with FEC. → G8

**Attenuator** — A device that reduces signal strength. Used to prevent receiver overload from nearby strong signals. → G4

## B

**Balanced modulator** — A circuit that combines carrier and audio to produce a double-sideband suppressed-carrier (DSB-SC) signal. The first stage in generating SSB. → G7

**Bandwidth** — The range of frequencies occupied by a signal. SSB ≈ 3 kHz, FM ≈ 10–15 kHz, CW ≈ 150 Hz, FT8 ≈ 50 Hz. → G8

**Battery (lead-acid)** — Minimum safe voltage for a 12V lead-acid battery is 10.5V. Below this, deep discharge damages the plates. → G6

**Beacon** — An unattended transmitting station used for propagation testing. Automatically identifies at regular intervals. → G1

**Beverage antenna** — A long, low, terminated wire antenna used for directional receiving on low HF/MF bands. Poor for transmitting. → G9

**Bleeder resistor** — A resistor across a power supply's filter capacitor that safely discharges stored energy when the supply is turned off. → G7

**BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor)** — A transistor controlled by base current. Operates in saturation (fully on) and cutoff (fully off) as a switch. NPN and PNP types. → G6

**BNC connector** — Bayonet-locking coaxial connector usable to about 4 GHz. → G6

## C

**Carson's Rule** — Bandwidth of an FM signal ≈ 2 × (peak deviation + maximum modulating frequency). → G8

**CEPT** — European agreement allowing US amateurs to operate in participating countries with their FCC license. Requires a CEPT permit. → G1

**Characteristic impedance** — The impedance a transmission line presents to a traveling wave, determined by the line's physical geometry. Most amateur coax is 50Ω. Window line is ~450Ω. → G9

**Class A amplifier** — Linear amplifier that conducts for the full 360° of the input cycle. Most linear, least efficient (~25%). → G7

**Class AB amplifier** — Conducts for slightly more than 180°. Good compromise between linearity and efficiency (~50%). Standard for SSB. → G7

**Class B amplifier** — Conducts for exactly 180°. More efficient than A/AB. Used in push-pull configurations. → G7

**Class C amplifier** — Conducts for less than 180°. Most efficient (~80%) but nonlinear. Only suitable for FM or CW, not SSB. → G7

**Control operator** — The licensed amateur responsible for proper operation of a station. Must hold a license equal to or higher than the privileges being used. → G1

**CQ** — General call to any station. "CQ DX" specifically calls distant stations. → G2

**Crystal filter** — A narrow-bandwidth filter using piezoelectric crystals. Common in IF stages for SSB selectivity. → G7

**CW (Continuous Wave)** — Morse code transmission. The narrowest common amateur mode (~150 Hz bandwidth). → G2, G8

## D

**dB (decibel)** — Logarithmic ratio of power. +3 dB = double, -3 dB = half, +10 dB = 10×. Used for gain, loss, and signal strength. → G5

**dBd** — Antenna gain relative to a half-wave dipole. → G9

**dBi** — Antenna gain relative to an isotropic radiator. dBi = dBd + 2.15. → G9

**D layer** — Lowest ionospheric layer, present only during daytime. Absorbs HF signals (especially lower frequencies). Disappears at night, improving nighttime HF propagation. → G3

**Diode** — A two-terminal semiconductor that conducts in one direction. Silicon threshold ≈ 0.7V, Germanium ≈ 0.3V. → G6

**Dipole** — A half-wave antenna fed at the center. Length (feet) = 468 / f(MHz). Free-space feed impedance ≈ 73Ω. → G9

**Director** — A parasitic Yagi element shorter than the driven element, placed in front. Focuses radiation forward. → G9

**DSP (Digital Signal Processing)** — Software-based signal filtering. Can implement noise reduction, notch filters, and bandwidth control. → G4

## E

**E layer** — Ionospheric layer at about 110 km altitude. Supports sporadic-E propagation. Maximum single-hop distance ≈ 1,200 miles. → G3

**EFHW (End-Fed Half-Wave)** — A half-wave antenna fed at one end. Very high feed impedance (thousands of ohms), requires a matching transformer. → G9

**ERP (Effective Radiated Power)** — Transmitter output minus feed line loss plus antenna gain. The actual power radiated toward the horizon. → G1, G5

## F

**F2 layer** — Highest and most important ionospheric layer for long-distance HF propagation. Maximum single-hop distance ≈ 2,500 miles. Present day and night. → G3

**FEC (Forward Error Correction)** — Error correction where redundant data is sent so the receiver can fix errors without retransmission. Used in FT8 and some digital modes. Contrast with ARQ. → G8

**Feed line** — Cable connecting a transmitter to an antenna. Coax (50Ω) and ladder/window line (450Ω) are most common. Loss increases with frequency. → G9

**Ferrite core** — Magnetic core material for inductors and transformers. The frequency range is determined by the mix (material type), not core size. → G6

**FT8** — Popular weak-signal digital mode using 8-FSK modulation. ~50 Hz bandwidth, 15-second transmit periods, can decode signals as low as -20 dB SNR. → G2, G8

## G

**Gamma match** — An impedance matching method for Yagi antennas. Advantage: the driven element doesn't need to be insulated from the boom. → G9

**GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter)** — A protective device that trips when it detects current leakage to ground. Required in wet locations. → G0

**Gray line** — The twilight zone between day and night on Earth. Enhanced propagation along this path due to ionospheric transition effects. → G3

**Ground plane** — A system of radials beneath a vertical antenna that provides a low-loss RF return path. Elevated verticals need 3–4 radials; ground-mounted need 32+. → G9

## H

**Hairpin (Beta) match** — A shorted transmission line stub used to match a Yagi's driven element to 50Ω feed line. → G9

**Harmonic** — An integer multiple of the fundamental frequency. Harmonics from transmitters must be filtered to prevent interference. Multiband antennas have poor harmonic rejection. → G8, G9

## I

**I/Q (In-phase / Quadrature)** — Two signals 90° apart used in SDR to represent the complete signal (amplitude and phase). → G7

**IF (Intermediate Frequency)** — A fixed frequency to which the received signal is converted for filtering and amplification. → G7

**Impedance** — Opposition to AC current flow, combining resistance and reactance. Z = √(R² + X²). Measured in ohms. → G5

**Intermodulation** — Spurious signals created when two or more signals mix in a nonlinear device. Third-order intermod is the most problematic. → G8

**Inverted V** — A dipole supported by a single center mast with legs sloping downward. Slightly more omnidirectional than a flat dipole. → G9

## K

**K-index** — A 3-hour measurement of geomagnetic disturbance (0–9 scale). K ≤ 2 is quiet (good HF). K ≥ 5 means a geomagnetic storm (poor HF). → G3

## L

**Ladder line** — See Window line. → G9

**LED (Light-Emitting Diode)** — A diode that emits light when forward biased. Schematic symbol is a diode with arrows pointing away. → G6

**LiFePO4** — Lithium iron phosphate battery chemistry. Safe, stable, long cycle life. Popular for portable amateur operations. → G4

**Link budget** — Calculation of all gains and losses in a radio path: transmitter power + antenna gains - feed line losses - path loss. → G8

**Log-periodic antenna** — A wideband directional antenna with elements that vary logarithmically in size. Covers a 2:1+ frequency range but has less gain per element than a Yagi. → G9

## M

**MOSFET** — Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistor. Controlled by voltage on an insulated gate (virtually no gate current). → G6

**MPE (Maximum Permissible Exposure)** — FCC limits on RF exposure to humans. Depends on frequency, power, distance, and duty cycle. Must evaluate if station exceeds threshold. → G0

**MUF (Maximum Usable Frequency)** — The highest frequency that will be reflected by the ionosphere for a given path. Above the MUF, signals pass through into space. → G3

## N

**NEC (National Electrical Code)** — Specifies wire gauges for household circuits: 20A → AWG 12, 15A → AWG 14. Tested in G0. → G0

**Noise blanker** — A receiver circuit that gates out short-duration impulse noise (ignition, power lines). → G4

**Notch filter** — A filter that rejects a very narrow band of frequencies. Used to eliminate a single interfering signal (e.g., a nearby carrier). → G4

**NVIS (Near Vertical Incidence Skywave)** — Propagation technique for covering 0–300 miles using nearly vertical signals reflected by the F layer. Requires a low horizontal antenna on 40m or 80m. → G3, G9

## O

**Op-amp (Operational Amplifier)** — A high-gain differential amplifier with inverting (−) and non-inverting (+) inputs. Schematic symbol is a triangle. → G7

**Oscillator** — A circuit that generates a continuous AC signal. Requires amplification and positive feedback. → G7

**Oscilloscope** — A test instrument that displays signal waveform (voltage vs. time). Used for two-tone tests, checking modulation, measuring distortion. → G4

## P

**PACTOR** — An HF digital mode combining ARQ error correction with adaptive modulation. Used in Winlink for email over radio. → G2, G8

**PEP (Peak Envelope Power)** — The average power during one RF cycle at the crest of the modulation envelope. PEP = V_peak² / (2R). Caution: if given peak-to-peak voltage, divide by 2 first. → G5

**Phase** — The angular position of a waveform at a given time. Phase differences are key in I/Q processing, SSB generation, and antenna phasing. → G7, G8

**Polarization** — The orientation of the electric field. Horizontal dipoles = horizontal polarization. Verticals = vertical polarization. Cross-polarization causes signal loss. → G9

**Product detector** — A mixer circuit that combines the IF signal with a BFO to demodulate SSB or CW. → G7

**PSK31** — Phase-shift keyed digital mode with ~31 Hz bandwidth. Keyboard-to-keyboard communication using Varicode encoding. → G2, G8

## Q

**Q (Quality factor)** — The ratio of energy stored to energy dissipated per cycle in a reactive component or circuit. High Q = narrow bandwidth. Low Q = wide bandwidth. → G5

## R

**RACES (Radio Amateur Civil Emergency Service)** — An emergency communication service activated by civil defense authorities. Participants must be registered with the local RACES organization. → G2

**Reactance** — Opposition to AC current from capacitors (X_C = 1/(2πfC)) or inductors (X_L = 2πfL). Measured in ohms but does not dissipate power. → G5

**Reflector** — A parasitic Yagi element longer than the driven element, placed behind it. Reflects energy forward. → G9

**Resonance** — The frequency where inductive and capacitive reactance are equal and cancel. At series resonance, impedance is minimum (purely resistive). f = 1/(2π√(LC)). → G5

**RMS (Root Mean Square)** — The effective DC equivalent of an AC signal. V_RMS = V_peak × 0.707. → G5

**RTTY (Radioteletype)** — A digital mode using FSK (Frequency Shift Keying) with a ~170 Hz shift. One of the oldest digital modes. → G2, G8

## S

**S-meter** — A signal strength indicator. Each S-unit = 6 dB = 4× power change. S9 is the reference level. → G4

**SDR (Software Defined Radio)** — A radio where traditional hardware functions (filtering, demodulation, modulation) are performed in software. Uses I/Q data streams. → G7

**Skin effect** — At higher frequencies, current flows in a thinner layer on the conductor surface, increasing effective resistance. Major cause of feed line loss at HF/VHF. → G9

**SMA connector** — A small threaded coaxial connector usable to 18+ GHz. → G6

**Speech processor** — A device that increases average transmitted power without increasing peak power. Improves readability in noise. → G4

**Sporadic E** — Unpredictable, short-lived E-layer ionization that can support VHF propagation over long distances. → G3

**SSB (Single Sideband)** — A modulation mode that transmits only one sideband (upper or lower). Most bandwidth-efficient phone mode (~3 kHz). Convention: LSB below 10 MHz, USB above. → G2, G8

**SWR (Standing Wave Ratio)** — A measure of impedance mismatch. SWR = Z_big / Z_small. 1:1 is perfect. Higher SWR = more reflected power. → G9

## T

**Third-party traffic** — Messages sent on behalf of a non-licensed person. Allowed domestically; internationally only with countries that have third-party agreements. → G1

**Transformer** — Transfers energy between circuits via magnetic coupling. Turns ratio sets voltage transformation; impedance transforms as the square of the turns ratio. → G5, G7

**Trap** — A parallel LC circuit inserted in an antenna element to enable multiband operation. At resonance, it isolates sections of the antenna. → G9

**Two-tone test** — A test using two non-harmonically related audio tones to check SSB transmitter linearity. Viewed on an oscilloscope. → G4

**Type N connector** — A threaded coaxial connector usable to about 10 GHz. More durable than BNC. → G6

## V

**Vacuum tube** — An older amplification device still used in high-power amateur amplifiers. Advantages: handles high power and is tolerant of high SWR. → G6

**Varicode** — A variable-length binary encoding used in PSK31. Common letters (e, t) use fewer bits than rare ones (z, q). → G8

**VE (Volunteer Examiner)** — A licensed amateur who administers FCC exams. Three VEs required per session. Can only administer exams for classes below their own license level. → G1

**Velocity factor** — The speed of an electromagnetic wave through a medium compared to free space. Coax is typically 0.66–0.82. Affects the physical length of transmission line stubs and antennas. → G9

**VOX (Voice Operated Transmit)** — Automatic transmit/receive switching triggered by voice. Needs anti-VOX to prevent speaker audio from triggering transmit. → G2

**Volunteer Monitor Program** — Replaced the Official Observer program. Volunteer Monitors observe the amateur bands and report rule violations to the FCC. → G2

## W

**Waterfall display** — A time-frequency plot where the X-axis is frequency, the Y-axis is time, and color/brightness indicates signal strength. Standard display for digital mode operation. → G8

**Window line** — A parallel-conductor feed line with ~450Ω impedance. Very low loss compared to coax. The "windows" reduce dielectric material. → G9

**Winlink** — A global email-over-radio system using PACTOR, VARA, or packet connections to relay email via HF or VHF. → G2

## Y

**Yagi-Uda antenna** — A directional antenna with a driven element plus parasitic reflector(s) and director(s). Reflector is longest, director is shortest. Adding directors/boom length increases gain. → G9

## Z

**Zener diode** — A diode designed to conduct in reverse at a specific breakdown voltage. Used for voltage regulation. Schematic symbol has a bent cathode bar. → G6
